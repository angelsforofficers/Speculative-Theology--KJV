## **Ahaziah Age and Reign Explained**

The confusion concerning this subject is that one passage says Ahaziah was 22 when he began to reign where in another place, it’s recorded that he was 42 years old, and both passages agree on Ahaziah reigning one year in Jerusalem. So which is it? And the answer is both \- as recorded.

**2 Kings 8:26** \- “Two and twenty years old was Ahaziah when he began to reign; and he reigned one year in Jerusalem. (...)"

**2 Chronicles 22:2** \- “Forty and two years old was Ahaziah when he began to reign, and he reigned one year in Jerusalem. (...)"

To start with, we have to rule out the possibility of copyist error, keeping in mind it’s the Lord who takes responsibility for the accurate copying and preservation of scripture.

**2 Timothy 3:16** \- “All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness:”

**Psalms 12:6-7** \- “\[6\] The words of the LORD are pure words: as silver tried in a furnace of earth, purified seven times. \[7\] Thou shalt keep them, O LORD, thou shalt preserve them from this generation for ever.“

### **The Short Explanation**

In 2 Kings 8:26, Ahaziah first reigned as regent over Israel at 22 years of age, where in the account in 2 Chronicles 22:1-2, he reigns as king over Judah starting at 42 years of age.

Shortly after Ahaziah began reigning as king over Judah, Joram was wounded fighting the Syrians and Ahaziah took up the regency again as acting ruler of Israel while Joram was recovering from his injuries, reigning for one year over Judah and Israel.

- 2 Chronicles recounts the histories of the kings of Judah in detail where first and second Kings focus on Israel, so 2 Chronicles omits the reign at 22 years of age and only mention when Azariah ruled as king over Judah for 1 year in Jerusalem.

### **Ahaziah’s Reign Over Israel**

**2 Kings 8:26**  
“Two and twenty years old was Ahaziah when he began to reign; and he reigned one year in Jerusalem. And his mother's name was Athaliah, the daughter of Omri king of Israel.”

- Athaliah was the daughter of Ahab so in context of this passage, she was Omri’s granddaughter \- which still makes her his “daughter” by the standards of the text  
- The same is done in mentioning that Ahaziah was Jehosaphat’s son in 2 Chronicles 22:9 (grandson) though he’s also the son of Joram king of Judah (2 Chronicles 22:1).

This establishes his right to “reign” over either kingdom, Israel through Athaliah and Judah through Jehoram, but it doesn’t specify which one he reigned over. The account in 2 Kings chapter 8 also adds the detail that Omri was king over Israel, where 2 Chronicles 22 only mentions Omri in passing, further evidencing that the account in 2 Kings is referring to Israel specifically.

**2 Chronicles 22:1-2**  
“\[1\] And the inhabitants of Jerusalem made Ahaziah his youngest son king in his stead: for the band of men that came with the Arabians to the camp had slain all the eldest. So Ahaziah the son of Jehoram king of Judah reigned.  
\[2\] Forty and two years old was Ahaziah when he began to reign, and he reigned one year in Jerusalem. His mother's name also was Athaliah the daughter of Omri.”

This record provides specifically where and how he reigned \- as king in Jerusalem over Judah for one year (after the inhabitants thereof made him king).

This is the only place it mentions him reigning “as king”, whereas 2 Kings just says “he began to reign” in general, which could have been in a position of regency over the land while Ahab was in the field fighting against the Syrians at the time.

You see the same kind of thing happening elsewhere in 2 Kings:

**2 Kings 8:25**  
“In the twelfth year of Joram the son of Ahab king of Israel did Ahaziah the son of Jehoram king of Judah begin to reign.”

**2 Kings 9:29**  
“And in the eleventh year of Joram the son of Ahab began Ahaziah to reign over Judah.”

One says eleven and the other says twelve \- within one chapter of each other. But begin to reign where and in what capacity?

**2 Kings 8:25** is over Israel while Joram king of Israel was incapacitated due to being injured in battle by the Syrians (Ahaziah was present for this battle as well) where **2 Kings 9:29** tells you he reigned over Judah specifically.

So 11 years after Joram began to reign over Israel, Ahaziah began to reign over Judah (and he reigned one year in Jerusalem).

During the 12th year of Joram's rule in Israel, Ahaziah stepped in to oversee the kingdom as a regent. Since Joram had been injured during a conflict with the Syrians and was recovering in Jezreel, Ahaziah managed the affairs of Israel while continuing his own reign over Judah.

The distinction between Joram’s eleventh and twelfth years might simply be a matter of days or weeks, depending on how close the timing was to the anniversary of his coronation. Much like the transition from late December to early January, the two dates could represent a very brief chronological gap despite belonging to different numbered years of his reign.

**2 Kings 8:28-29**  
“\[28\] And he went with Joram the son of Ahab to the war against Hazael king of Syria in Ramothgilead; and the Syrians wounded Joram.  
\[29\] And king Joram went back to be healed in Jezreel of the wounds which the Syrians had given him at Ramah, when he fought against Hazael king of Syria. And Ahaziah the son of Jehoram king of Judah went down to see Joram the son of Ahab in Jezreel, because he was sick.”

Now add this to 2 Kings 8:26:

**2 Kings 8:25**  
“In the twelfth year of Joram the son of Ahab king of Israel did Ahaziah the son of Jehoram king of Judah begin to reign.”

- As regent over Israel in place of Joram who was wounded

**2 Kings 8:26**  
“Two and twenty years old was Ahaziah when he began to reign; and he reigned one year in Jerusalem. And his mother's name was Athaliah, the daughter of Omri king of Israel.”

The claim that Ahaziah reigned over Israel is further substantiated by 2 Kings 8:25, a passage that explicitly confirms his authority to rule and connects his actions to the northern kingdom. Notably, 2 Kings 9:29 describes events occurring prior to those in 2 Kings 8:25, yet specifically designates his reign as being over Judah.

Based on these records, 2 Kings 8:26 can be understood as the point when Ahaziah first began his reign over Israel. While the author focuses on Ahaziah's subsequent reign in 2 Kings 8:25, the initial portion of 2 Kings 8:26 \- preceding the semicolon \- references his first period of rule.

The narrative then returns to his specific oversight of Israel, a one-year tenure in Jerusalem that took place while Joram, king of Israel, was recovering from battle wounds sustained against the Syrians and when Ahaziah was 42 years of age, following his appointment as king over Judah.

### **Ahaziah’s Age Relative to His Father Jehoram**

Assuming that every "began to reign" statement marks a consecutive, sole kingship leads directly to biological impossibilities \- such as a son being older than his father.

By correctly applying the principle of co-regencies, a highly common practice used by the Davidic dynasty to secure succession and train heirs, the chronologies in 2 Chronicles align perfectly.

To make the scriptural timeline biologically sound while honoring the exact ages recorded in the King James Bible, we can establish the following estimated age ranges:

- As his firstborn, Jehoram was likely born when Jehoshaphat was in his early prime, between 18 and 25 years old.  
- Because Ahaziah is explicitly identified as Jehoram’s "youngest son" (2 Chronicles 22:1), Jehoram had to have been older when Ahaziah was born \- realistically between 30 and 45 years old.

If we estimate that Jehoram was 35 when his youngest son Ahaziah was born, we can map out a cohesive timeline. In this model, Jehoram's "32 years of age" marks his elevation to co-regent alongside Jehoshaphat, and his "8 years" marks his final period of sole reign in Jerusalem before his death.

### **Chronological Timeline**

Jehoshaphat Fathers Jehoram. Jehoram's Age: 0

- Jehoshaphat is roughly 18 to 22 years old when his firstborn son, Jehoram, is born.  
- Jehoram Begins Co-Regency  
- Jehoram's Age: 32

**2 Chronicles 21:5:** "Jehoram was thirty and two years old when he began to reign (...)"

To secure the kingdom, Jehoshaphat elevates his firstborn to co-regent. Jehoram is 32\. Jehoshaphat is in his early 50s.

Jehoram Fathers Ahaziah. Jehoram's Age: \~35

- Ahaziah, the youngest son, is born to Athaliah and Jehoram.  
- Jehoram's Sole Reign Begins  
- Jehoram's Age: \~69

**2 Chronicles 21:5:** "(...) and he reigned eight years in Jerusalem."

Jehoshaphat's period of supreme authority ends (having reigned his 25 years as sole sovereign), and Jehoram transitions from a junior co-regent to the sole ruler of Judah for the final 8 years of his life.

- Ahaziah Begins to Reign  
- Jehoram's Age: \~77 | Ahaziah's Age: 42

**2 Chronicles 22:2:** "Forty and two years old was Ahaziah when he began to reign, and he reigned one year in Jerusalem."

Jehoram dies at roughly 77 years of age. Ahaziah (born when Jehoram was 35\) is 42 years old as he takes the throne in his father's stead.

This framework also harmonizes with 2 Chronicles 20:31, which states that Jehoshaphat "was thirty and five years old when he began to reign, and he reigned twenty and five years in Jerusalem."

Just as Jehoram had overlapping phases of rule, this 25-year period defines Jehoshaphat's era of independent, sole sovereignty.

The scriptural accounting often isolates the years a king held supreme authority from the years they spent as a junior co-regent to their predecessor or as an elder patriarch allowing their son to govern. By recognizing that these reigns overlapped, the ages of 32, 8, and 42 harmonize seamlessly.

### **Be Careful with Joram / Jehoram in 2 Kings**

Joram is the king of Israel, and Jehoram is the king of Judah. There are two different Jehoram’s mentioned in these passages.

- One was called Jehoram and Joram interchangeably, and was the king of Israel through Ahab’s bloodline  
- One was called Jehoram and Joram interchangeably, who was the ruler over Judah

You know there are two different people being delt with because they die in different ways, mentioned in two different passages.

Jehoram / Joram king of Israel dies in 2 Kings 9:22-24  
Jehoram / Joram king of Judah dies in 2 Chronicles 21:16-19, also mentioned in 2 Kings 8:24
