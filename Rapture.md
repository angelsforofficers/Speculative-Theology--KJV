Current biblical chronology suggests the earth is approximately 6,000 years old, calculated by combining 4,000 years of Old Testament history with 2,000 years of the current era. Drawing a parallel to the Genesis creation where the Lord rested on the seventh day, it is understood that the 7,000th year will initiate a 1,000-year period of rest and sabbath known as the millennial reign.

Following this reign, the earth will undergo a renovation by fire to become new, aligning with the Hebrew significance of the number 8 as representing new beginnings; thus, the year 8,000 signifies a fresh start for the earth.

This framework views the 6,000 years of human history as living by faith, while the 7,000th year transition to living by sight under the reign of Jesus Christ, fulfilling a millennium of sabbaths that echo the original creation.

### **Passages Commonly Confused Pertaining to the Rapture and Second Advent**

**Isaiah 26:19-21**  
“\[19\] Thy dead men shall live, together with my dead body shall they arise. Awake and sing, ye that dwell in dust: for thy dew is as the dew of herbs, and the earth shall cast out the dead.”

* This is the Rapture \- talking about saved people because the “dead” at the end of the millennial reign go forth to judgment \- they won’t be singing at the white throne judgment

“\[20\] Come, my people, enter thou into thy chambers, and shut thy doors about thee: hide thyself as it were for a little moment, until the indignation be overpast.”

* This is the Tribulation / Time of Jacob’s Trouble

“\[21\] For, behold, the Lord cometh out of his place to punish the inhabitants of the earth for their iniquity: the earth also shall disclose her blood, and shall no more cover her slain."

* This is the Second Advent, and end of the millennium
